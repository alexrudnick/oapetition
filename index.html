If USENIX, the Association for Computational Linguistics and NIPS (among others) can commit to Open Access, then the ACM can also find a way. Computer Science research is too important to hide behind a paywall, and in any case it rightly belongs to the public.

Individual signers may make particular pledges for any or all of the following until all of the ACM Digital Library is Open Access: (a) not publish in ACM venues (b) not review for ACM venues (c) not buy/renew ACM membership, and (d) continue to raise the issue to anybody who will listen.
